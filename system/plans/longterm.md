## Phase Map

### Phase 1: Strength Base (NOW — Jul 2026)
**Goal**: Complete 30-week program. Squat 120 x5, Bench 105 x5. Build deadlift to 180kg+.
**Focus**: Strength, linear periodization, 4 days/week.
**What gets de-prioritized**: Cardio, calisthenics skills, aesthetics arm work (limited by elbow).
**Why**: Without a strength base, OLY technique is dangerous and calisthenics goals are unreachable.

### Phase 2: OLY Technique + Strength Maintenance (Aug 2026 — mid 2027)
**Goal**: Learn snatch and C&J. Maintain squat/bench/DL gains. Start weighted pull-up work.
**Focus**: Technical skill, 3-4 days/week (OLY-biased). Keep heavy compound work 2x/week.
**Why**: OLY transitions require dedicated technique time. Better now than after more years of pure strength patterns.

### Phase 3: Parallel Development (2027 — 2028)
**Goal**: Calisthenics strength (muscle up, weighted dips/pull-ups). Continue OLY. Aesthetics work.
**Focus**: Hybrid: OLY 2x, heavy compounds 2x, calisthenics skill work.
**What begins**: Deliberate arm work (if elbow allows), aesthetics focus.
**Why**: Relative strength work complements OLY. At this bodyweight, 80kg weighted dips is achievable with the strength base built.

### Phase 4: Cardio / Athleticism Layer (2028 onwards)
**Goal**: VO2 max, sprint speed, running. These are long-term health and athleticism markers.
**Why last**: Cardio volume interferes with strength building when both are maximized. Better to build the strength base first, then layer in serious cardio work. Resting HR and HRV will improve naturally with lighter cardio even during strength phases.

## Goal Prioritization (when goals conflict)

When time, energy, or recovery forces a choice:
1. **Longevity** > everything else (never sacrifice joints or long-term health)
2. **Current program phase goal** > long-term goals (finish what is started)
3. **Athletic capability** > aesthetic outcomes (form follows function)
4. **Strength** > cardio during current phase (Phase 1 logic)
5. **Enjoyment and consistency** > optimal programming (a workout you do beats one you skip)

---

## Golden Rules (non-negotiable)

These override any plan, any block, any session:

1. **Longevity first** — never trade joint health for a number
2. **Max 4 training days per week** (lifestyle constraint, non-negotiable)
3. **Deload every 5 weeks** or immediately when body signals it (load index HIGH, HRV crash, sleep collapse)
4. **No training through sharp joint pain** — modify or skip, never push through
5. **Sun exposure daily** — tracked as a health metric, not optional
6. **Sleep is a performance metric** — below 6h average over 7 days is a flag for session modification
7. **No arm isolation until elbow clears** — direct curls and tricep isolation suspended until severity < 2
